30-Day Marketing Strategy — Abroaducate Launch
The one goal: 50 paying users in 30 days
Everything below serves that goal. Not followers, not signups — paying users who bought credits.

Week 1 (Days 1-7): Announce and activate
Day 1 — Launch announcement email to newsletter Send a personal email to your 8,341 subscribers. Not a digest. A personal note from you:

Subject: I built something for you

"For the past year I've been building a platform to help African students find affordable university programs in Europe. Today it's live. Here's what it does..."

Show the 3 free credits. Link to abroaducate.com. One CTA: "Try it free."

Keep it short. No images. Plain text feels personal.

Day 1 — Twitter/X announcement thread Write a 5-tweet thread:

"I just launched Abroaducate — a platform that helps African students find affordable programs in Europe. Here's what it does 🧵"
Show the scholarship matching (screenshot)
Show the AI strategy feature (screenshot)
Show the credit pricing ($4.99 for 20 credits)
"3 free credits when you sign up. Link in bio."
Pin it to your profile.

Days 2-3 — Follow up with Gumroad buyers Email your ebook buyers separately. They already paid you money — they're your warmest leads. Tell them the platform is live and their ebook knowledge is now actionable on the platform.

Days 4-5 — Post on Twitter daily

Day 4: "Here's a German university with 0 tuition that most people don't know about" → screenshot from the platform → "Find it on Abroaducate"
Day 5: "I matched 679 scholarships to 2,597 programs so you don't have to" → show the scholarship radar
Days 6-7 — Engage with replies and DMs Anyone who replies to your launch thread or emails you — respond personally. These are your first potential paying users. Ask them what they're applying for. Show them their program on the platform.

Week 2 (Days 8-14): Content and conversion
Daily Twitter content (pick one per day):

"The cheapest Master's programs in Germany right now" (list from the platform)
"How the DAAD scholarship works — and who actually gets it"
"I analyzed 679 scholarships. Here's what most applicants get wrong"
"Lithuania has free Master's programs. Here's how to apply"
"What a 3.5 GPA gets you in Europe vs the US"
"The SOP mistake that kills most applications"
"France has 0-tuition universities. Here's the catch"
Each post ends with a soft CTA: "I built a tool that matches you to these programs automatically. Link in bio."

Mid-week email to registered users who haven't used their credits Query Supabase: users who signed up but have 3 credits (never spent any). Send them a nudge:

Subject: You have 3 free credits waiting

"You signed up but haven't tried the AI strategy yet. Here's what it does..."

One CTA: "Use your free credits"

Weekend: post a case study Write a Twitter thread showing a real example: "I ran the AI strategy on a Computer Science program at TU Munich. Here's what it found..." — use your own account as the test case. Show the output. This is the most powerful content you can post.

Week 3 (Days 15-21): Double down on what's working
By now you'll know which content got the most engagement. Double down on that format.

Check your numbers:

How many of the newsletter subscribers registered?
How many registered users spent credits?
How many bought credits?
If the newsletter → registration conversion is low: the email wasn't compelling enough. Send a second email with a different angle.

If registration → credit usage is low: the onboarding is broken. Watch PostHog session recordings to see where people drop off.

If credit usage → purchase is low: the pricing page needs work, or people don't understand the value.

Start posting on LinkedIn Repurpose your best Twitter threads as LinkedIn posts. LinkedIn has a different audience — more professionals, more people considering postgrad. The study abroad content performs well there.

Reach out to 5 study abroad communities Find Facebook groups, Reddit communities (r/gradadmissions, r/studyabroad), Discord servers for African students. Don't spam — join, contribute, then mention the platform when relevant.

Week 4 (Days 22-30): Push for the 50
Email the newsletter again A second email, different angle. First email was "here's what I built." Second email is "here's a real student who used it" — or if you don't have that yet, "here's what the AI strategy actually outputs."

Run a limited-time offer "First 100 users who buy credits get 25% more credits." This creates urgency without discounting the price. You can do this manually in Stripe by creating a coupon code.

DM your most engaged Twitter followers Look at who liked and retweeted your launch thread. DM them personally: "Hey, I saw you engaged with my launch post — did you get a chance to try it? Happy to walk you through it." 10 personal DMs will convert better than 1,000 broadcast tweets.

Ask for your first testimonial Find one user who used the platform and got value. Ask them for a 2-sentence quote. Post it on Twitter. Social proof at this stage is worth more than any feature.

What NOT to do this month
Don't run paid ads yet. You don't know your conversion rate well enough to spend money on traffic.
Don't build new features. Ship what you have to users first.
Don't post more than once a day on Twitter. Quality over quantity.
Don't obsess over follower count. Obsess over credit purchases.

The numbers that actually matter (in priority order)

1. Credits spent — the most important signal Source: Supabase SQL

SELECT action_type, COUNT(*) as count
FROM credit_transactions
WHERE created_at > NOW() - INTERVAL '7 days'
  AND amount < 0
GROUP BY action_type
ORDER BY count DESC;
This tells you which features people actually used. If nobody spent credits, the platform didn't deliver value yet. If people spent credits, you have product-market fit signal.

2. New signups this week Source: Supabase SQL

SELECT COUNT(*) as new_users
FROM auth.users
WHERE created_at > NOW() - INTERVAL '7 days';
Compare this to your existing 5,107 users. How many new people joined because of the launch?

3. Activation rate — signups who actually used a credit Source: Supabase SQL

SELECT COUNT(DISTINCT ct.user_id) as activated_users
FROM credit_transactions ct
JOIN auth.users u ON u.id = ct.user_id
WHERE ct.created_at > NOW() - INTERVAL '7 days'
  AND u.created_at > NOW() - INTERVAL '7 days'
  AND ct.amount < 0;


This is the percentage of new signups who used the product. Industry benchmark for a new SaaS is 20-30%. Anything above 10% in week 1 is solid.

4. Revenue — did anyone pay? Source: Supabase SQL

SELECT COUNT(*) as purchases, SUM(ABS(amount)) as total_credits_bought
FROM credit_transactions
WHERE created_at > NOW() - INTERVAL '7 days'
  AND action_type LIKE 'STRIPE_%';
Even 1 paying customer in week 1 is a meaningful signal. It means someone valued the product enough to spend money.
