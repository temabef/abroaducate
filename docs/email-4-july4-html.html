Portugal just joined the platform

Portugal doesn't get talked about enough in the study abroad conversation. It should.

Tuition at Portuguese public universities runs €950–€1,500 per year for international students, a fraction of what UK or US schools charge. The country has been expanding English-taught programs aggressively, especially in Lisbon and Porto. And as a Schengen country, your student visa gives you freedom of movement across 27 European countries.

There's also a growing tech and startup scene. Lisbon hosted Web Summit for years for a reason. If you're in tech, business, or engineering, Portugal is worth a serious look.

One more thing: the US visa situation

I've been getting messages about this. US student visa approvals for African applicants have become significantly harder and less predictable in 2026. Processing times are longer. Rejection rates are up. Some students who were admitted to US programs are now reconsidering.

Europe doesn't have this problem. Germany, Portugal, France, Sweden: student visas are straightforward, processing is predictable, and once you're in, you're in the Schengen zone.

If you or someone you know is weighing the US against Europe right now, the calculus has shifted. Europe is the smarter path in 2026.
