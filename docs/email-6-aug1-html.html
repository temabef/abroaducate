Sweden abolished tuition fees for EU students in 2010. For everyone else, fees apply.

That's the headline most people stop at. But there's a second part that changes everything for African students specifically.

Sweden has two major scholarship programs that cover 100% of tuition and living costs for non-EU students. They are well-funded, actively recruiting from Africa, and significantly underused because most applicants don't know they exist or don't apply strategically.

The Swedish Institute Scholarship for Global Professionals (SISGP)

This is the big one. It covers full tuition, a monthly living allowance of SEK 11,000 (roughly $1,000), travel grant, and insurance. It's for Master's students from eligible countries, and most African countries are on the list.

What the Swedish Institute is looking for is specific: they want people with at least 3,000 hours of documented work experience who have demonstrated leadership and a clear plan to create impact back home. This is not a scholarship for fresh graduates with strong grades. It's for people who have done something and can articulate what they'll do next.

SISGP at a glance

✓ Full tuition covered

✓ SEK 11,000/month living allowance (~$1,000)

✓ Travel grant included

✓ Open to most African countries

✓ Requires 3,000+ hours of work experience

Applications open: February 2027 (for autumn 2027 intake)

The Adlerbert Study Scholarship

Less known than the Swedish Institute scholarship, but worth knowing. The Adlerbert Research Foundation offers scholarships specifically for students at the University of Gothenburg and Chalmers University of Technology. It covers tuition and a living stipend.

If you're applying to programs at either of those universities, this is a direct funding path that most applicants overlook entirely.
