There's a number I want you to sit with for a second.

526

That's how many English-taught Master's programs exist in Germany right now, at zero tuition.

Not discounted. Not "affordable." Zero. German public universities abolished tuition fees in 2014. International students pay the same as German students, which is nothing. You pay a semester contribution of roughly €150–€350, and that fee usually includes a public transport pass for the entire state.

526 programs across Computer Science, Business, Engineering, Public Health, Law, Environmental Science, Development Studies, and more. The problem isn't that the programs don't exist. The problem is that most people don't know how to find the right one.

One myth worth killing:

"Don't you need to speak German to study there?"

For those 526 programs, no. They are fully taught in English. Lectures, exams, thesis, everything. Germany has been aggressively expanding English-taught programs specifically to attract international students.

You will need basic German for daily life (grocery shopping, dealing with the Ausländerbehörde), but the degree itself? English.
