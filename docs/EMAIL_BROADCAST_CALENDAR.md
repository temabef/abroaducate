## The Two Formats

**Format A — "One Thing"**
One specific, practical insight. Short (300–400 words). Ends with a soft CTA to the platform.
Goal: Get newsletter subscribers to register. Get registered users to use their credits.

**Format B — "Platform Update"**
What's new on Abroaducate. New scholarships, new programs, a feature. Shows the platform is alive.
Goal: Bring registered users back. Show newsletter subscribers the platform is growing.

## Rules for Every Email

1. **Plain text feel** — even with HTML design, write like a person, not a brand
2. **One CTA only** — don't give people 3 links to click
3. **Under 400 words** — if you can't say it in 400 words, cut it
4. **Reply-worthy** — end with a question or something that invites a response
5. **No "Buy credits now"** — never lead with a sales pitch; the platform sells itself when people use it
6. **Subject line rule** — specific beats clever. "Germany has 526 free programs" beats "Discover Europe"

---

## Tracking What Works

After each send, check Customer.io for:
- Open rate (target: >20%)
- Click rate (target: >2%)
- Replies (the most valuable signal)
- Unsubscribes (if >0.5%, the content missed)

If an email gets unusually high opens or clicks, do more of that format/topic.
